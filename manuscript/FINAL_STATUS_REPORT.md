## üéØ KEY FINDINGS SUMMARY

**Title:** High Consistency, Limited Accuracy: Evaluating Large Language Models for Binary Medical Diagnosis

**Main Finding:**
- Consistency: 99-100% (Qwen: perfect 100%)
- Accuracy: 48-51% (chance level)
- **Gap: ~50 percentage points** (consistency-accuracy dissociation)

**Key Insights:**
1. LLMs can be "reliably wrong" (consistent but inaccurate)
2. Systematic positive diagnosis bias (49-51 FP, 0-1 FN)
3. Prompt engineering ineffective (<3% change)
4. Inter-model agreement 98-99% (shared limitations)
5. Not ready for primary diagnosis, suitable for triage/support

**Novel Contribution:**
- First systematic evaluation of consistency vs accuracy in LLM diagnosis
- 1,200 predictions with rigorous checkpoint system
- Reveals fundamental limitation of general-purpose LLMs for medical classification
